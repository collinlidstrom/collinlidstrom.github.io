Reading List

Books I'm currently reading and recent recommendations.

Currently Reading

Team Topologies

By Matthew Skelton & Manuel Pais

A revolutionary guide to organizational design for modern software delivery, focusing on optimizing team structures and interactions.

Key Takeaways

"Conway's Law suggests the architecture of your systems will mirror your organization's communication structure."

"Team cognitive load is the limiting factor in software delivery performance."

  • Organization Design
  • DevOps
  • Team Structure

The First 90 Days

By Michael D. Watkins

Essential strategies for navigating new leadership roles and making successful transitions at all career levels.

Key Takeaways

"Transitions are periods of opportunity, a chance to start fresh and make needed changes in an organization."

"The actions you take in your first three months will largely determine your success or failure."

  • Leadership
  • Career Development
  • Management

Emotional Intelligence

By Mindful Minds Co

A comprehensive guide to understanding and developing emotional intelligence in professional and personal contexts.

Key Takeaways

"Self-awareness is the foundation of emotional intelligence."

"Emotional intelligence is twice as important as technical skills for job success."

  • Personal Development
  • Leadership
  • Psychology

Recently Finished

The Art of Scalability

By Martin L. Abbott & Michael T. Fisher

A comprehensive guide to scaling organizations, teams and systems in a structured and sustainable way.

Key Takeaways

"Scale is about people, organization and process as much as it is about technology."

"The AKF Scale Cube: Scale through splitting different ways (x-axis: cloning, y-axis: functional decomposition, z-axis: data partitioning)."

  • Architecture
  • Scalability
  • System Design

The Mythical Man-Month

By Frederick P. Brooks Jr.

Classic software engineering insights about team productivity and project management that remain relevant today.

Key Takeaways

"Adding manpower to a late software project makes it later."

"The second system effect: the tendency of small, elegant systems to be succeeded by over-engineered, bloated systems."

  • Software Engineering
  • Project Management
  • Team Dynamics

Multipliers

By Liz Wiseman

How the best leaders make everyone smarter by bringing out the intelligence and capabilities in others.

Key Takeaways

"Multipliers amplify the intelligence of others, while diminishers drain it."

"The best leaders are genius makers, not geniuses themselves."

  • Leadership
  • Management
  • Team Building

Radical Candor

By Kim Scott

A framework for providing honest feedback while maintaining strong relationships and building great teams.

Key Takeaways

"Care personally while challenging directly."

"Guidance should be given immediately, privately and in person when possible."

  • Leadership
  • Communication
  • Management

The Phoenix Project

By Gene Kim, Kevin Behr & George Spafford

A novel about IT, DevOps and helping your business win through a story of a struggling company's transformation.

Key Takeaways

"The Three Ways: Flow, Feedback and Continuous Learning."

"IT work is never done; projects are never completed, they evolve."

  • DevOps
  • IT Management
  • Digital Transformation

The Unicorn Project

By Gene Kim

A parallel novel to The Phoenix Project focusing on developer experience and the five ideals of software development.

Key Takeaways

"The Five Ideals: Locality, Focus, Flow, Joy and Improvement."

"Developer productivity is not about individual performance but about reducing system-level bottlenecks."

  • DevOps
  • Software Development
  • Digital Transformation
